What is Diabetes?

  • It's when the body cant produce or properly use insulin.
  • Insulin is responsible for regulating the Sugar in the blood.
  • Too much sugar in the blood can cause organ and nerve damage.
  • 11 million Canadians are living with some form of diabetes.
  • That is about 1/3 of all Canadians.

Types of diabetes

Type 1

  • It's an autoimmune disease.
  • It prevents production of insulin.
  • Develops in childhood, but can sometimes develop in adulthood
  • Around 10% of all diabetes cases

Type 2

  • Insulin produced isn't used properly or isn't in sufficient quantity.
  • It can be managed with proper diet and exercise, however may also require medication.
  • Mostly appears in adulthood, but can sometimes develop in childhood.
  • About 90% of all diabetes.

Gestational Diabetes

  • Is a temporary form of diabetes.
  • Occurs during pregnancy.
  • Somewhere between 3-20% of pregnant woman develop it.

Risks of having diabetes

  • Kidney disease
  • Retinopathy
  • Stroke
  • Anxiety
  • Nerve damage
  • Amputation
  • Erectile Dysfunction

Risk Factors

  • Overweight/Obese, "Chance to develop diabetes doubles for every 20% increase over desirable body weight."
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Family hisory
  • High blood pressure
  • Low levels of HDL cholesterol

Symptoms of diabetes

  • Extreme thirst
  • Weight loss
  • Hunger
  • Exhaustion
  • Skin problems
  • Slow to heal
  • Increased trips to the washroom
  • Yeast infection
  • Numbness of the extremitites
  • Nausea
  • Vomitin
  • Frequent infections
